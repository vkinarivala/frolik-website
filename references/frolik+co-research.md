Here’s the direction I’d take for Frolik + Co: build a premium, light, editorial interior-design website where kids’ spaces are the signature strength, but the studio is still clearly positioned for homes, studios, commercial spaces, and other interiors.

1. Strategic positioning

Core positioning

Frolik + Co designs warm, playful, highly functional interiors for homes, kids’ spaces, studios, and everyday life in Ahmedabad.

The site should not feel like a generic “turnkey interior designer in Ahmedabad” website. That space is crowded. Frolik’s advantage is that it already has credible proof in children’s interiors: The Architects Diary has covered Frolik + Co kids’ room projects like The Monochromatic Room, The Coral Joy, and The Purple Play Room, with details around neutral tones, natural materials, bunk beds, slides, storage, study areas, rounded corners, and spaces designed to feel playful without becoming chaotic.      

The broader Ahmedabad interior market is packed with generalist competitors. Houzz lists more than 1,100 Ahmedabad interior pros, MagicBricks presents interior services through cost comparisons and budget-led discovery, while players like Livspace and Design Jadugar sell process, timelines, warranties, quality checks, and end-to-end execution.        

So the website should say:

We do all kinds of interiors, but we bring a special understanding of children, families, softness, play, storage, colour, and everyday usability.

That is much more ownable than “best interior designer in Ahmedabad.”

2. Competitive landscape and opportunity

Local competitor pattern

Ahmedabad competitors mostly fall into four buckets:

Segment	Examples	What they sell	Gap Frolik can own
Large turnkey / modular	Livspace, HomeLane-type players, Krios	Convenience, catalogues, warranties, pricing, fast install	Less personal, less boutique, less emotionally rich
Premium local interior studios	Malvi Gajjar, 9M Design Studio, Sanchi Shah	Luxury homes, villas, offices, turnkey execution	Often adult-home focused, not deeply child-centric
Architecture-led boutique studios	Studio Aakruti, NLine, Circle Design	Craft, legacy, architecture, materiality, slow living	More serious/mature tone; less playful family/kids ownership
Kids-room SEO/service pages	Fadia Architects, Krios, Bharat Interior	Kids playroom, beds, study units, storage, themes	Often functional/generic, not editorial or brand-led

Malvi Gajjar positions around architecture, interiors, commercial spaces, and turnkey projects; 9M Design Studio positions as a luxury interior firm for refined homes, offices, commercial interiors, 3D visualization, and turnkey execution; Studio Aakruti uses a boutique luxury “legacy” narrative with Vastu, heritage, and founder involvement; Circle Design uses a calmer “soft, purpose-driven living” tone with craft and story.        

Kids-specific competitors exist, but many are service-page led. Fadia has a “Kids Play Room Designer in Ahmedabad” page; Krios has kid-room offerings around beds, study units, wardrobe-cum-study units; Bharat Interior talks about child-friendly materials, ergonomic furniture, adaptability, and storage.      

Opportunity for Frolik: become the most tasteful, premium, portfolio-led kids-space and family-interior studio in Ahmedabad, while still showing capability in full homes and commercial/studio interiors.

7. Visual and UI direction from the logo

The logo is strong: geometric, playful, soft, and not overly decorative. The website should borrow that language.

Palette

Use the logo colours, but add warm neutrals.

* Mint / aqua: #BAE0DF
* Coral: #EF9777
* Soft coral / border tone: #DFA08C
* White: #FEFEFE
* Warm ivory: #FAF7F2
* Soft clay: #D8A08A
* Deep text: #2F3433
* Muted sage: #BFCFC4

Typography direction

Use one expressive display typeface and one clean sans.

Good combinations:

* Fraunces + Manrope
* Recoleta-style serif + Inter
* Canela-style display + Neue Haas / Satoshi
* DM Serif Display + Work Sans for a free/simple route

The headings can be slightly playful, but body text should be very clean.

UI style

* Lots of white space.
* Large project photography.
* Soft geometric shapes inspired by the logo.
* Coral border moments.
* Mint content panels.
* Rounded image masks, but not too childish.
* Project cards with very little text.
* Subtle hover animations: shapes slide, image gently zooms, coral line appears.
* Use “+” as a recurring brand motif because the logo includes it.

Avoid

* Loud cartoon colours.
* Heavy black luxury theme.
* Too many gradients.
* Generic stock interiors.
* Overuse of “best interior designer.”
* Overly technical contractor language.
